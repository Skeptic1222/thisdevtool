All Five Calculation Modes Explained

1. X% of Y — Find a Percentage of a Number

This is the most common percentage calculation. The formula is: Result = (X / 100) × Y. For example, 15% of 200 = 30. This mode is useful for calculating tips at a restaurant, discounts on prices, tax amounts, commission earned, and any scenario where you need a fraction of a whole.

2. X is What % of Y — Find the Percentage Rate

Use this when you know two numbers and want to know the percentage relationship. The formula is: Percentage = (X / Y) × 100. For example, "30 is what percent of 150?" = 20%. This is useful for calculating test scores, completion rates, market share, and efficiency ratios.

3. Percentage Change from X to Y

This calculates how much a value has grown or shrunk as a percentage. The formula is: Change = ((Y - X) / |X|) × 100. A positive result means an increase; a negative result means a decrease. For example, going from $40 to $50 is a 25% increase. Use this for price changes, stock returns, population growth, and year-over-year comparisons.

4. Increase or Decrease a Number by a Percentage

This mode applies a percentage to adjust a base value. For increases: Result = X × (1 + Y/100). For decreases: Result = X × (1 - Y/100). For example, increasing 400 by 25% gives 500. This is practical for salary raises, price markups, discount pricing, and inflation adjustments.

5. Fraction to Percentage

Convert any fraction (numerator/denominator) directly to a percentage. The formula is: Percentage = (Numerator / Denominator) × 100. For example, 3/8 = 37.5%. This is useful for converting test scores, ingredient ratios, probability fractions, and statistical proportions into easy-to-communicate percentages.

Frequently Asked Questions

To find X% of Y, multiply Y by X and divide by 100. For example, 25% of 200 = (25 × 200) / 100 = 50. Use the "X% of Y" mode in this calculator and enter your values.
Percentage change = ((New Value − Old Value) / Old Value) × 100. A positive result means an increase; a negative result means a decrease. For example, going from 80 to 100 is a 25% increase.
To find what percentage X is of Y, divide X by Y and multiply by 100. For example, 30 is what percent of 120? (30 / 120) × 100 = 25%. Use the "X is what % of Y" mode.
To increase a number by a percentage: New Value = Original × (1 + Percent/100). For example, increasing 500 by 20% = 500 × 1.20 = 600. To decrease: New Value = Original × (1 − Percent/100).
Divide the numerator by the denominator and multiply by 100. For example, 3/4 = (3 ÷ 4) × 100 = 75%. Use the "Fraction to %" mode and enter the numerator and denominator.
